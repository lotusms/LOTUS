Why Does My Website Code Matter?

Have I mentioned technology is always moving forward? There is a reason why I keep repeating myself. I can't stress enough how important this is and here is the secret many web design and development companies will not tell you - You may not need an entire website overhaul! That's right! One look at your website and any company, in hopes of making as much money as possible, will suggest an entire new website.

This may not be needed in all cases. Sometimes is just as simple as optimizing the problem area and you've got yourself a really good website! Think of it this way, if you have a flat tire, do you trade your entire car in? Probably not. If your car is so old and worn out that it has many problem areas: engine is faulty and needs upgraded, interior is not equiped with latest gadgets such as GPS or Seat/Steering Wheel Heating, and your paintjob leave much to be desired; then you may consider trading it in for a new one.

Let's look at these upgrades and optimization services individually and remember, as long as you need only one of these, you should consider this solution versus a entire new site.

Deprecated Code

Old code or deprecated code hinders your website from performing according to new standards compatible with browsers. For instance:

  • Table formatting
  • Old DOCTYPE
  • Outdated jQuery Versions
  • Outdated CSS Libraries
  • and more...

Responsive Web Design

If your website was built prior 2010, there is a strong posibility that it is not a Responsive Web Design. In other words, the website does not adjust to screen sizes to allow for mobile devices to render your site in a format that visitors can read easily and interact with your site without having to pinch/zoom or scroll horizontally. View this site in a desktop, tablet, and phone, now view your website. Does it display in the same fashion?

User Experience (UX)

Having a website that creates frsutration and increases cognitive friction between the design and the user is a sure way to get them to click away or never come back again. By optimizing your User Experience, we can introduce psychological concepts that improve the user's experience while in your application. They shouldn't have to think, worry, or guess anything. And all clicks should eventually lead to a phone call.
