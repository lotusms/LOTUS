What You Need To Know About SEO

Google is, undoubtedly, the most used search engine in the world. If your website does not appear in Google’s search results, your website is practically failing you. Having a great looking website when no one sees it is like screaming in an empty room. This is not ideal and it gives you a false sense of security making you think you are on the right track. On-page SEO (On-Page Search Engine Optimization), is the work behind the curtains. In other words, it is not something you would see, but Google and other search engines can read it. On-Page SEO is the way your website communicates with search engines so that they can understand the website content and provide it to the users as a good source of information.

You must remember, Google has no idea what images, text, and videos are. All they see is code. This is why it is important your website is coded for both audiences: the users and the search engines.

SEO is a lengthy process that can take several months before you see any significant results – sometimes years. But it all starts with a website that is coded with On-Page SEO. The rest depends on the Off-Page search engine optimization and web marketing efforts on your part.

Being able to rank in the most frequented search engine in the world (Google) depends on a number of factors. The following are a few things to consider and perhaps a good list to read whenever you feel defeated by your website not moving up as quickly as you would like it to.

  • Popularity – your product must have a favorable degree of popularity.
  • Competition – your competition may be also emploting SEO strategies. Amp It Up!
  • Content – your content must be dense enough to add value to your website – Blog Away!
  • On-Page SEO – have I beaten this to the ground enough?
  • Domain – Your domain msut be at least one year old to gain trust from Google.
  • Viral Marketing – Use social networks to attract traffic to your website
  • Freshness – Your content must change frequesntly or Google will render your site stale
  • Unique Content – DO NOT, under any circumstances, plagiarize. Make sure your content is yours.
  • Ownership – Use royalty-free images sparringly, it is best to use your own images. Own your brand.
  • No DIYs – DIY websites that allow you to build your website for free lack in On-Page SEO. You get what you pay for.

Rule of thumb is, your website is more than color and pretty images. When you build a site without taking search engines in consideration you are basically building a site that hardly anyone will see unless you direct them there by providing the website address or name. This is not the ultimate goal. The goal is to have users who have never heard of your company visit your website becasue you ranked on Google’s first page when they searched your product keyword. On-Page SEO does this for you.

To escalate through the ranks of search engines make sure you follow the basics

  • Register your domain for longer than 1 year.
  • Make sure your website is built with On-Page SEO. No DIYs!
  • Be very consistent in social networks and promote your website.
  • Change your website content periodically.
  • Do not take part in any black hat SEO schemes. This will get your website banned.
  • Remain knowledgeable with the current changes in SEO. It changes very frequently.
