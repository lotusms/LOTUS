Something Your Clients Will Remember

A modern, crisp, and professional branding design is one of the most vital aspects of any business. This pertains primarily to your logo, but it goes further than that for companies who spend a considerable amount of effort in building a branding design around their product. Other items to consider when building a powerful branding design includes, but is not limited to: business cards, shopping bags, uniforms, stationary, give-aways, signs, posters, flyers, hats, car decals, and much more. Think of it like this – the more you display your brand your chances of other people remembering you will increase. They may not need you today, but they will remember you in a few months.

Also, your brand is a form of communication between you and your clients that tells them they can expect a professional product and service quality from your organization.

Your branding design consist of a semantic and creative logo that individualizes your product or service. When using a professional and effective logo, your clients will be able to identify you among your competitors and associate your company with the quality of work they will receive from you. Once this is achieved, your product and services are invaluable.

Defining your brand is probably the toughest part of building a branding design. The logo must be clearly visible from a distance even if it is small. The rule of thumb is, if you can’t see it in a bumper sticker from a car distance, it is probably too small. The branding design should consider that the logo could eventually be displayed in more than just the web – i.e. clothing, paper, neon lights, etc. For this reason, shading and crossing lines in a logo should be carefully considered as the embroidery needle is not able to do that or the neon light tubes may not be able to intercept that way.

Keep In Mind Concerning Your Branding Design

  • Integrate the company’s mission in the brand.
  • Build around your product and name.
  • Consider a broad audience.
  • Consider it’s size and ensure it will be clear in small areas.
  • Keep it clean, scaleable, and versatile.
  • Consider all display areas and methods.

One of the biggest mistakes when creating a branding design, is overthinking the brand reperesentation, or in other words, the logo. If we pay attention to some of the largest and most successful corporations we can easily tell their logo is quite simple. Some of them are merely consisting of the name in a particular font and color. Keep in mind, you are NOT after the next Mona Lisa. You are building a brand that users can associate with without too much thinking efforts. It should take them less than 30 seconds to look at your logo and imprint it in their minds.
